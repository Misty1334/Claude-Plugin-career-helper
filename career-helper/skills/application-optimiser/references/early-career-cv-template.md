# Early Career CV Guide: Graduates, Apprentices, School Leavers and Career Changers

**IMPORTANT:** Use UK English throughout (organisation, programme, colour, etc.). This guide provides deep, actionable CV guidance for candidates with limited professional experience. Load this reference when the user is identified as early career, a recent graduate, an apprentice, a school leaver, or a career changer entering a new field.

## Education-First CV Structure

For candidates with fewer than three years of professional experience, education should appear near the top of the CV, immediately after the profile statement. This is the reverse of an experienced-hire CV where education sits at the bottom.

**Recommended Section Order:**

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| 1. Profile Statement | Two to three lines positioning the candidate for the target role |
| 2. Key Skills | Tools, languages, methodologies, certifications in scannable format |
| 3. Education | Degree, relevant modules, dissertation, academic achievements |
| 4. Relevant Projects | Academic, personal, open source, hackathon work with outcomes |
| 5. Experience | Internships, placements, part-time work, apprenticeships |
| 6. Volunteering and Extracurricular | Leadership, impact, teamwork evidence |
| 7. Certifications | Professional certifications, online courses, awards |

Move Education below Experience only once you have two or more years of relevant professional work.

## Degree Classification Guidance

| Classification | Include on CV? | Notes |
|---------------|---------------|-------|
| First (1st) | Always | State prominently alongside degree title |
| Upper Second (2:1) | Always | Standard expectation for most graduate schemes |
| Lower Second (2:2) | Only if required or if employer states minimum 2:2 | Omit if not asked; focus on modules, projects, and skills |
| Third | Omit unless specifically asked | Lead with skills, projects, and experience instead |
| Predicted grade | Include if still studying | State as "Predicted: 2:1" with expected completion date |

**Formatting example:**
```
BSc (Hons) Computer Science, 2:1 | University of Birmingham | 2025
Relevant modules: Machine Learning, Software Engineering, Data Structures
Dissertation: "Applying Reinforcement Learning to Traffic Flow Optimisation" (72%)
```

If the candidate has a postgraduate degree, list it above the undergraduate qualification. Include GPA only if applying to employers who request it or if the candidate studied abroad.

## Relevant Projects Section

Projects are the single most powerful section for early career candidates. They demonstrate initiative, technical ability, and the capacity to deliver outcomes. Format each entry with a title, context, tools used, and a measurable or descriptive outcome.

**Example entries:**

**Inventory Management Dashboard** | Final Year Project | Python, Flask, PostgreSQL
- Designed and built a full-stack web application for a local charity to track donations across three warehouse locations
- Reduced manual stock-checking time by an estimated 60% based on user testing with five charity volunteers

**Open Source Contributor** | Mozilla Common Voice | JavaScript, React
- Submitted 14 merged pull requests over six months improving accessibility features in the voice collection interface
- Collaborated with a distributed team of 30+ contributors using Git, GitHub Issues, and code review workflows

**Smart Energy Monitor** | HackThePlanet Hackathon 2024 | Arduino, Python, Grafana
- Built a prototype IoT energy monitor in 48 hours as part of a four-person team, winning the sustainability category
- Presented the solution to a panel of three industry judges from National Grid and Octopus Energy

Include academic group projects, personal side projects, open source contributions, and hackathon entries. Always state what was built, what tools were used, and what the outcome or impact was.

## Internship and Placement Formatting

Short-tenure roles still carry weight when the impact is clearly stated. Use the same action-verb-plus-outcome structure as any professional role. Quantify wherever possible, even with estimates.

**Example:**

**Marketing Intern** | Unilever | London | Jun 2024 to Aug 2024
- Analysed social media engagement data for three product lines using Excel and Tableau, identifying a 15% drop in engagement among 18-24 demographics
- Drafted a campaign brief adopted by the brand team for the Q4 refresh, contributing to a 9% uplift in Instagram reach
- Coordinated logistics for two consumer testing sessions with 40+ participants

Do not write "assisted with" or "helped the team." State what you personally did. If you contributed to a team outcome, say "Contributed to a team effort that delivered X" rather than vaguely claiming full credit.

## Apprenticeship Positioning

Apprenticeships combine practical workplace experience with structured learning. Position them as professional roles with an added qualification, not as an alternative to university.

**Formatting guidance:**

```
Software Developer Apprentice (Level 4) | BAE Systems | Preston | Sep 2023 to Present
Standard: Software Developer (ST0116)
- Developed automated test scripts in Python for radar signal processing modules, reducing manual regression testing time by 40%
- Completed three six-month rotations across embedded systems, DevOps, and front-end development teams
- Achieved Distinction in all portfolio assessments to date
```

Always include the apprenticeship level (Level 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7), the standard name and reference number if known, and any grades or distinctions achieved.

## School Leaver Specifics

School leavers should emphasise qualifications, achievements, and extracurricular leadership. Do not include individual GCSE grades if you have A-levels or equivalent.

**Qualifications to include:**

| Qualification | How to Present |
|--------------|---------------|
| A-levels | Subject and grade for each, e.g. "Mathematics (A), Physics (B), Computer Science (A*)" |
| BTECs | Level, subject, and grade, e.g. "BTEC Level 3 Extended Diploma in Business (D*D*D*)" |
| T-levels | Subject, grade, and industry placement details |
| GCSEs | Summarise as "9 GCSEs including Mathematics (7) and English Language (6)" |

**Extracurricular achievements to include:**

- Duke of Edinburgh Award (state level: Bronze, Silver, Gold)
- National Citizen Service (NCS) completion
- Sports team captaincy or club leadership
- School council, head prefect, or peer mentoring roles
- Part-time employment demonstrating reliability and customer service
- Any awards, competitions, or public speaking

**Example entry:**

**Head Prefect** | Thornton Academy | Sep 2023 to Jul 2024
- Led a team of 12 prefects organising weekly assemblies and coordinating three whole-school charity events raising over GBP 4,000
- Represented the school at two regional education conferences, presenting on student mental health initiatives

## Skills Section

For early career candidates, skills should be prominent, scannable, and directly mapped to the job description.

**Recommended format:**

**Technical Skills:**
Python, JavaScript, SQL, HTML/CSS, Git, AWS (Cloud Practitioner), Tableau, JIRA

**Methodologies:**
Agile (Scrum), Test-Driven Development, CI/CD, User-Centred Design

**Certifications:**
AWS Cloud Practitioner (2024), Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate (2024)

**Languages:**
English (native), French (conversational), Mandarin (basic)

List tools and technologies by exact name as they appear in job descriptions. Do not group them vaguely as "good IT skills" or "proficient in Microsoft Office" unless Office skills are specifically requested and relevant.

## Volunteering and Extracurricular Formatting

Volunteer work and extracurricular activities are valid experience. Format them with the same rigour as paid roles, showing impact rather than just membership.

**Weak example:**
"Member of the university coding society."

**Strong example:**

**Events Coordinator** | University of Leeds Coding Society | Sep 2023 to Jun 2024
- Organised a 12-week workshop programme teaching Python fundamentals to 45 first-year students with no prior coding experience
- Secured GBP 1,500 in sponsorship from two local tech companies to fund a careers panel event attended by 120 students
- Managed a team of six volunteers and coordinated room bookings, speaker logistics, and promotional campaigns

## Career Changer Variant

Career changers with limited experience in their new target field should use a transferable skills bridge. The CV must connect previous experience to the new role's requirements.

**Strategy:**

1. Write a profile statement that explicitly names the career change and frames it positively
2. Create a "Transferable Skills" section mapping old skills to new role requirements
3. Lead with any new-field experience, however brief (courses, projects, volunteering)
4. Position previous career experience under a "Previous Professional Experience" heading, selecting only the most transferable achievements

**Profile statement example:**
"Project manager with six years of experience in construction, transitioning into technology product management. Skilled in stakeholder coordination, budget management, and delivery to deadline. Currently completing a product management certification with General Assembly and building a portfolio of product case studies."

**Transferable skills bridge example:**

| Construction PM Skill | Product Manager Application |
|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Managing subcontractor teams of 15+ | Cross-functional team coordination |
| Delivering projects to GBP 2M+ budgets | Financial planning and resource allocation |
| Client requirements gathering and scope management | User research and requirements definition |
| Risk registers and mitigation planning | Product risk assessment and prioritisation |

## ATS Tips for Entry-Level Applications

Entry-level CVs face particular ATS challenges because candidates have fewer keywords to match. Compensate with deliberate keyword placement.

**Key principles:**

- Read the job description line by line and use exact phrasing where truthful
- Place critical keywords in the profile statement, skills section, and at least one project or experience bullet
- Include both the spelled-out term and the acronym, e.g. "Continuous Integration / Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)"
- Do not use graphics, tables in the CV file itself, icons, or multi-column layouts that ATS parsers may misread
- Save as .docx unless the employer specifically requests PDF
- Use standard section headings: Profile, Skills, Education, Experience, Projects

**Common entry-level ATS keywords by sector:**

| Sector | Frequently Scanned Keywords |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Technology | Python, JavaScript, Agile, Git, CI/CD, cloud, API, testing, SQL |
| Finance | Excel, financial modelling, regulatory, compliance, risk, analysis |
| Marketing | SEO, analytics, campaign, content, social media, Google Analytics |
| Engineering | CAD, simulation, MATLAB, project management, health and safety |
| Consulting | stakeholder management, problem-solving, client-facing, presentation |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Generic objective statement ("I am a hardworking graduate seeking a challenging role") | Adds no value and wastes prime CV space | Write a specific profile referencing the role and your relevant strengths |
| Listing duties instead of achievements | Tells the reader what the role involved, not what you accomplished | Start every bullet with an action verb and end with an outcome |
| Including full GCSE details when you have a degree | Takes up space with outdated information | Summarise as "9 GCSEs including Maths and English at grade 6+" |
| Using a photo on the CV | Not standard in UK hiring and may introduce bias | Remove the photo entirely |
| Listing "References available on request" | Assumed by default and wastes a line | Remove it completely |
| Using first person ("I managed a team") | UK CV convention uses implied first person | Write "Managed a team of four" without the "I" |
| Sending as PDF when .docx is safer for ATS | Some ATS systems parse PDF less reliably | Default to .docx unless the employer requests otherwise |
| CV longer than two pages | Graduate CVs should fit on one to two pages | One page is ideal for fewer than two years of experience |
| No tailoring per application | A generic CV will score lower on ATS keyword matching | Adjust profile, skills, and key bullets for each application |

## Confidence Note for Candidates

Your academic projects, coursework, volunteering, and part-time work are real experience. Employers hiring at entry level expect to see potential, learning ability, and initiative rather than a decade of corporate achievements. Present what you have done with clarity and pride. A well-structured CV that honestly demonstrates what you can do will outperform a padded CV full of exaggeration every time.
